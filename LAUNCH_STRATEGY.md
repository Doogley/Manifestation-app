# Already Mine — 30-Day Launch Strategy

A launch plan for a solo developer with no existing audience. Everything here is
sized to be doable by one person in 1–2 hours/day alongside a life. The brand
voice in all copy below matches the app: quiet, certain, unhurried. Never hype.
The app never begs — neither does the marketing.

**One rule that governs everything below:** the app's whole thesis is "what's
meant for you is already on its way." The marketing must feel like that too.
Post like someone who knows the app is good, not someone who needs you to
download it.

## SECTION 2 — LAUNCH DAY

Launch on a **Tuesday or Wednesday** (App Store features and press skew
midweek; weekends are dead for launches). Have the app approved and in
"Manually release this version" state *before* launch day so you control the moment.

### Exact steps, in order

1. **Morning (7am):** Press "Release this version" in App Store Connect.
   Verify the store listing renders correctly on a real phone. Install the
   *live* App Store version yourself, create a fresh account, run one full
   loop (signup → onboarding → reveal → journal → subscribe with your real
   Apple ID if you want to verify billing, then refund/cancel).
2. **Check the plumbing:** Supabase dashboard open — watch signups arrive.
   Verify a sandbox/real purchase flips `is_paid_member`. Watch Vercel logs
   for `/api/summary` errors.
3. **8–9am:** Post the launch video (post idea #8) on TikTok and Instagram
   (Reel + Story with the link sticker). Pin it on both profiles.
4. **Personal circle, personally:** text/DM 15–30 actual humans who know you —
   individually, not a broadcast. Script: *"My app is live today. Would mean a
   lot if you downloaded it and told me honestly what you think. If you like
   it, a review on the App Store is the single most helpful thing."* Warm
   contacts convert ~10× better than strangers and seed your first reviews.
5. **Communities (give, don't spam):** post in 2–3 places where you're already
   a member or can write something genuinely useful — r/lawofattraction or
   r/Manifestation (check self-promo rules first; a "I built this because…"
   story post survives where a link-drop dies), Indie Hackers, a build-in-public
   thread on X if you have any presence there. Product Hunt is optional for a
   consumer wellness app — do it in week 2–3 as a second spike, not on day 1.
6. **Creator wave 1:** send the first 10 outreach messages (Section 4) — to
   micro creators you've already been *engaging with for the past week*
   (comment on their posts days before you DM; cold DMs from ghost accounts
   get ignored).
7. **Evening:** reply to every single comment, DM, and review. Screenshot the
   first nice user message (ask permission) → tomorrow's Story.

### How to ask for App Store reviews

- **In person/DM (launch week):** the direct ask in step 4 above. Aim for
  10–15 honest reviews in week 1 — that's enough for the listing to not look
  dead.
- **In app (build this in week 2–3):** trigger `SKStoreReviewController`
  (Capacitor plugin: `@capacitor-community/in-app-review`) at a *high* moment,
  not at launch: right after the streak-restored celebration, after marking an
  intention received, or on day 7 of a streak. Never after a paywall, never on
  first open. Apple caps the prompt at 3×/year per user, so spend it on your
  happiest moments.
- **Never** incentivize reviews (cinders-for-reviews would be a guideline
  violation). Never ask "only if you'd give 5 stars" in the app — but in a
  personal DM to a friend, "if you like it, review it" is fine.

## SECTION 3 — FIRST 30 DAYS

### The operating rhythm

**Daily (45–60 min):**
- Post 1 TikTok (batched Sundays; posting takes 5 min)
- Reply to every comment/DM on both platforms
- 15 minutes of genuine engagement: comment thoughtfully on 10 posts in your
  hashtags (this is how micro creators discover *you*)
- Check Supabase: signups, subscriptions, referral codes used
- Read every piece of in-app feedback (`feedback` table) — reply personally
  via email when there's an email to reply to

**Weekly (2–3 hrs, e.g. Sunday):**
- Batch-record next week's content; make 5 affirmation cards
- Send 10 new creator outreach messages + follow-ups from 5–7 days ago
- Write down: what post performed best this week? Make 2 variants of it.
- Ship one small improvement users asked for (you're a solo dev — "I built
  what you asked for in 4 days" is itself a marketing story; post it)

### Creator outreach timing

- **Days 0–7:** wave 1 — 10 micro creators (1k–50k) you pre-warmed.
- **Days 7–14:** wave 2 — 20 more micros + follow-ups to wave 1. By now you
  have real user reactions and (hopefully) reviews to reference.
- **Days 14–21:** wave 3 — first 5 mid-tier attempts (50k–500k) using template
  2, now with social proof ("X users in the first two weeks").
- **Days 21–30:** double down on whoever responded. One creator who posts
  twice beats five who post once. Offer top responders a custom
  category-themed post idea ("your audience is all abundance-mindset — here's
  what the Abundance track looks like").

### How to use the referral code system (and its current reality)

What exists in the app today: a **referral code field at signup** (free text,
stored on `profiles.referral_code`) and an **admin panel counting signups per
code** (5× logo tap → Referral Stats). What does *not* exist yet: automatic
attribution of *subscriptions* to codes, and any payout mechanism.

So run it manually — which is fine and normal at this scale:

1. Assign each creator a short memorable code: their handle (e.g. `LUNA`).
   Tell them to have followers enter it at signup.
2. **Commission (20%):** monthly, run one SQL query in Supabase joining
   `referral_code` against `is_paid_member = true` to count paying subscribers
   per code. Pay 20% of collected subscription revenue (after Apple's cut, be
   explicit about that in the agreement: ~20% of net) via PayPal/Venmo.
   Keep a simple spreadsheet ledger per creator.
3. Be honest with creators that tracking is code-at-signup — followers must
   actually enter it. Put the code in their caption AND have them say it out
   loud in the video.
4. **Build later (week 3–4, ~a day of work):** a `redeemed_by` report you can
   screenshot to creators monthly. Transparency is what keeps creators posting.
5. Watch the admin Referral Stats panel weekly — a code suddenly spiking tells
   you which creator/audience actually converts, which is exactly who to
   invest in.

### Ongoing content strategy (after the first 2 weeks)

Settle into 4 recurring formats — recurring formats compound because followers
learn to expect them:

1. **Mantra Monday** (weekly) — post yours, ask theirs.
2. **Affirmation cards** (2–3×/week) — the saveable filler.
3. **Feature moments** (1–2×/week) — grace period, received intentions,
   monthly reflection, day-N library. Rotate.
4. **Builder diary** (1×/week) — what you shipped, what users said, real
   numbers if you're comfortable. This is the content that gets you creators,
   press, and other developers rooting for you.

Repost every user screenshot/testimonial (with permission) to Stories. UGC is
free and converts better than anything you make.

### Key metrics (the numbers that matter, in order)

Track weekly in one spreadsheet row. Ignore vanity follower counts.

1. **Installs → signups** (App Store Connect installs vs. Supabase `profiles`
   count) — if there's a big gap, first-open is broken or auth is scaring
   people off.
2. **Day-1 / Day-7 retention proxy** — you have `last_visit_date` and
   `streak_count` in Supabase: what % of accounts created 7+ days ago have a
   streak ≥ 2? ≥ 7? *This is the number that decides everything.* A retention
   problem makes marketing pointless; fix product first.
3. **Free → paid conversion** (`is_paid_member` / total profiles): healthy for
   this category is 2–5% early. Also watch *when* they convert — if everyone
   converts at day 30 (the cap), the cap is your best salesman; if nobody
   reaches day 30, see #2.
4. **Trial-to-paid rate** (App Store Connect, once trials are on): below ~40%
   means the trial length or onboarding-to-value gap is wrong.
5. **Referral code share of signups** — tells you creator ROI vs. organic.
6. **Content:** saves + shares per post (not likes), profile-visits → link
   taps. One save is worth ten likes.
7. **Reviews:** count and average. Respond to every review, especially bad ones.

### When to consider paid advertising

**Not in the first 30 days.** Paid ads amplify a conversion machine; they
can't build one. Turn ads on only when ALL of these are true:

- Day-7 streak-retention above ~20% of signups
- Free → paid conversion ≥ 2% and stable for 2+ weeks
- You know your rough LTV (annual plan ≈ $15 net of Apple's cut; if a
  subscriber costs you more than ~$7–10 to acquire, you lose)
- You have 5+ organic videos that already performed — those become your ad
  creative (Spark Ads on TikTok / boosted Reels), because they're pre-validated

When you do start: $10–20/day on TikTok Spark Ads on your best organic video,
targeted broad, for 2 weeks. Measure installs-per-dollar against organic
before scaling a cent. Apple Search Ads on your own brand name ("already
mine") is cheap insurance worth turning on earlier — pennies to own your own
search term.

## SECTION 5 — PRICING STRATEGY

### Launch pricing: keep what you have

**$2.99/month · $21.49/year ($1.79/mo equivalent, "Save 40%")** is the right
launch price. Reasoning:

- The category anchors are Bloom ($49.99/yr), I Am ($19.99/yr), Motivation
  ($59.99/yr), Stoic (~$40/yr). At $21.49 you're priced as "affordable but
  real" — above impulse-junk, below premium-brand. Right for an app with no
  reviews yet.
- Your paywall design already pushes annual (default-selected, Save 40%
  badge). Annual is what you want: 12 months of retention risk transferred to
  you up front, and LTV you can spend against later.
- **Do not lower prices to grow.** In this niche price signals seriousness;
  $0.99 reads as junk. Growth problems in month 1 are retention/awareness
  problems, never price problems.

One consideration before launch, not after: $21.49 is an odd anchor. $19.99
("under $20") or $24.99 both test better psychologically than $21.49. If
you're going to change it, change it *now* while you have zero subscribers to
grandfather. After launch, leave it alone for 90 days minimum.

### Free trial: 7 days on annual only

- Configure in App Store Connect/RevenueCat: **7-day free trial on the annual
  plan only.** Monthly stays trial-less (monthly *is* the low-commitment
  option; a trial on it just delays revenue).
- Why 7 days: your product's magic is cumulative — streak forming, library
  growing, a week of journal entries. 3 days isn't enough to feel that. 14
  days delays the payment past the emotional peak. 7 days = one full weekly
  loop including a Mantra Monday.
- Your paywall must then show "7 days free, then $21.49/year" on the CTA —
  Apple requires accurate trial disclosure, and clarity converts better anyway.
- Update the ToS "Free Trial" section timing language if needed (it's already
  written generically — good).

### When to consider price changes

- **90+ days in, and only with data.** If trial-to-paid is >55% and day-30
  retention is strong, you're underpriced — raise the annual to $29.99 for
  *new* users (existing subscribers keep their price automatically unless you
  opt into Apple's price-increase flow — don't, at this scale).
- If conversion is fine but volume is tiny, price isn't the lever — awareness is.
- If people subscribe and cancel within the trial en masse, price isn't the
  lever — the day-2-to-7 experience is.
- Never A/B price by region manually at this scale; just use Apple's automatic
  price tiers per storefront.

### Lifetime deals & promotional pricing

- **Lifetime: don't sell it publicly at launch.** It caps your best users' LTV
  exactly when you don't know your retention curve. Two exceptions worth
  making: (1) creators and early supporters get lifetime *comped* free — costs
  you nothing, buys goodwill and content; (2) if you ever need a cash spike
  (e.g. month 4–6), a 72-hour "founding member lifetime — $49.99, one weekend
  only, never again" to your existing free users converts fence-sitters
  without training the market to wait for sales. If you say "never again,"
  mean it.
- **Promotional pricing:** use App Store **promotional offers/win-back offers**
  (via RevenueCat) rather than changing the list price — e.g. lapsed
  subscribers get 3 months at 50%. New Year's (Jan 1–14) is this category's
  Black Friday: run your one big annual promo then ("New year, new practice —
  first year $14.99"), not in your launch month.
- **Referral users:** the 20% creator commission already discounts your
  margin; don't stack a user-side discount on top. If a creator wants
  something for their audience, give their code an *extended 14-day trial*
  (configurable as an offer) instead of a price cut — costs you days, not
  dollars.

## The one-page version

Week −1: set up IG+TikTok, batch 7 videos, warm up 10 micro creators.
Day 0 (Tue/Wed): release, launch video, 20 personal DMs, 10 creator DMs, reply to everything.
Weeks 1–4: 1 TikTok/day, 10 outreaches + follow-ups/week, ship user requests, watch day-7 streak retention above all.
Money: keep $2.99/$21.49 (or round to $19.99 now), add 7-day trial on annual, pay creators 20% monthly by hand, no ads until retention and conversion earn them.
